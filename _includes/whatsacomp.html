We will be covering the major components of a computer which are as follows:

  • Motherboard
  • Central Processing Unit(CPU)
  • Random Access Memory(RAM)
  • Hard Disk Drive(HDD)
  • Solid State Drive(SSD)
  • Interface Cards(WiFi, Sound, Video)

     These components are only a few of the devices you may or may not come into contact with when working. It will be our goal to provide you with as much exposure to different devices to provide you the ability to take on more work with a very capable proficiency. Now let's begin!

     Our first component to review is a Motherboard. What is a Motherboard? A motherboard is a device that ties together all of the parts that allow your device to work as expected. A simple way to imagine this as something a bit more relatable is relating a computer to our body. The motherboard would be like our central nervous system being the device that is used for a traffic system where commands come from one location and go to another. Certain parts are like highways while others are like one way streets. A motherboard starts out as what is called a Printed Circuit Board (PCB). Now I know you are wondering what a PCB is and whether we will be covering that. We currently only need to know what a PCB is at this time as the PCB subject has a capability to become much greater than familiarizing you with what’s a computer. If you would like to read more briefly on the PCB subject visit this wikipedia site at PCB’s.

     On the motherboard you have slots and cabling to allow for upgrades of certain devices. We will be reviewing a desktop Personal Computer (PC) motherboard and a laptop PC motherboard only. Let's start with a desktop PC motherboard. You have either seen these and have used them or believe only gamers and techy people use these devices to enhance their experience. This is both true and false. This statement is true as in the desktop PC allows more versatility, upgradable features, and modifications that are not possible on a basic laptop, however, this is false as there are many other people and professions that use these desktop PC’s to allow for better capabilities to perform their jobs such as home designers and graphic designers.
